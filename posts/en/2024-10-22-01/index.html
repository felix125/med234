A Medical Twist: How an X-ray Instantly Relieved Back Pain!

Author： Felix Chang
Published on： October 22, 2024

One year, I took Zhuzhu to visit my grandmother in Sanxing, Yilan. The area was so rural and the atmosphere so relaxed that we ended up playing along the way. We took turns piggybacking each other, enjoying the blue sky, green waters, mountains, forests, and farmlands. Everything felt so pleasant. But as the saying goes, “joy can turn to sorrow,” and this was probably referring to the accident that followed.

During our play, when I jumped onto Zhuzhu’s back, she suddenly screamed in pain and could barely speak clearly. “It hurts! It hurts…,” she said. “You… you’re too heavy…” and “My back…”

I was startled, but I was only in my 70-kilo range, which might have been too much for her as a woman. We quickly said a brief hello to my grandmother and hurried back to Taipei.

For the next week, Zhuzhu applied hot compresses, but the pain didn’t subside. I also tried massaging her, but it didn’t seem to help much.

“We should probably see a doctor,” she said. “Good idea, it’s been a week, and there’s a good orthopedic clinic right next to us at Zhenxing Hospital. Let’s go and register, can you make it to the hospital?” I replied. “Yeah! Let’s go now.”

At Zhenxing Hospital, we registered for Dr. Zhu, who later also treated me for a ruptured ligament in my right foot, but that’s another story. Dr. Zhu was a humorous person. In the consultation room, although there were various orthopedic tools, the atmosphere was unexpectedly relaxed. After a few quick checks on Zhuzhu’s back, he recommended an X-ray. Once the results were ready, he made his final diagnosis.

Fortunately, it wasn’t very crowded that day, and the X-ray was done quickly. Dr. Zhu looked at the X-ray and, while laughing, said, “Wow, look at that curve! This is the most beautiful spine I’ve ever seen—it could be used as a textbook example!”

“Really?” Zhuzhu replied, both surprised and delighted.

“Your spine looks perfectly fine. However, you might want to see a gastroenterologist. You have some backed-up stool. I’ll prescribe some painkillers for you. Take them if needed, and just rest at home.”

I think Dr. Zhu didn’t realize that the moment Zhuzhu saw the X-ray, her back pain vanished completely. The “seeing is believing” effect kicked in, and the placebo effect was triggered by the doctor’s humorous explanation and the stark reality of the X-ray image, instantly curing her pain.

However, things aren’t always so lucky. As pointed out by a study in The British Medical Journal, researchers compared 210 patients with lower back pain who underwent X-rays upon admission with another group of 211 patients who didn’t have X-rays. Even though both groups received the same treatment, the patients who had their X-rays taken felt like they were treated properly but didn’t experience much improvement. In fact, their pain continued for a longer period. This could be because seeing the damaged discs on the X-ray might have worsened the perception of the problem, activating a negative placebo effect1.

I can only say that Zhuzhu and I were really lucky. If we had been in the situation described by the journal, I’m not sure how long Zhuzhu’s back pain would have lasted. In medical treatment, it’s not just about the physical condition—how we handle the psychological aspect is also crucial. Our health may not only be about our physical state but also the subtle interaction between our mind and body.

Footnotes

  1. Kendrick, Denise et al.: Radiography of the lumbar spine in primary care patients with low back pain: randomised controlled trial. In: British Medical Journal 322, 2001, S. 400-405 ↩
